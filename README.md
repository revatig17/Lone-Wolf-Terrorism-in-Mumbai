# Lone-Wolf-Terrorism-in-Mumbai

Over the past few years, countries across the globe are facing the menace of lone wolf terrorist attacks. These attacks perpetrated by individuals with limited or no organizational support has begun to pose major challenge to security forces across the world. Studies have attributed the cause behind lone wolf terrorist incidents as loneliness, mental illness, societal isolation, indoctrination of vulnerable youths from radical groups etc. Additionally, it has also been noticed that petty criminals who have committed crimes such as robbery, manslaughter etc. have also been seen susceptible to radicalism when they come in contact with jailed terrorists.  Over the years organizations like ISIS, al-Qaeda have shifted their method of planning and execution from conventional group attacks to attacks committed by a single perpetrator. 

In the last few years there have been several incidents of lone wolf attacks across many countries. For instance, on 14th August 2018, [a car crashed into a barrier outside the Houses of Parliament](https://www.bbc.com/news/uk-45180120) in London. It was the second incident at this location, with the [first one having taken place in the year 2017.](https://www.bbc.com/news/uk-39359158) 

Earlier a gunman who [was let out on day-release](https://www.theguardian.com/world/2018/may/30/belgian-gunman-killed-person-a-day-before-deadly-liege-attack) from jail on 28th May 2018, attacked and shot two policewomen the next day in the city of Liege in Belgium. The attacker was killed by local armed forces after a gun battle broke out. 

Within the discourse of terrorism studies, the phenomenon of lone wolf terrorism is not new. In the year 1995, the Oklahoma City attacks wherein a truck driven by a US citizen exploded a truck bomb killing 168 people. 

With reference to London, in its article, Financial Times quoted Mr. Pantucci as saying that “the city’s biggest advantage is the [joined-up nature](https://www.ft.com/content/790a3b2c-9721-11e7-8c5c-c8d8fa6961bb) of policing, intelligence and emergency response.” Additionally, with the help of International Protect and Prepare Security Office (IPPSO), a management consultancy that specializes in counterterrorism measures, Government in UK has also take steps to [probe effective means](https://www.ippso.co.uk/) to counter incidents of lone wolf terrorism in crowded places. 

Over the years, social media has played the most crucial role in creation of extremism all around the world. It is a well-known fact that such extremist organizations use social media to establish connections with youth in order to radicalize them. Recognizing this aspect, European Union (EU) has taken is to put a restrain on social media platforms in order to tackle with extremist content. The EU finds it essential to take immediate actions on social media platforms to curb illegal propaganda for so as to decrease the cases of lone wolves.

To ensure that such threats are nipped in the bud, new sets of strategy, use of advanced technologies, and greater boots on ground of security personnel and public participation is highly essential. 

Although these attacks are more commonly seen in the West, it is an undeniable fact that they can occur repeatedly in India as well. Due to the prevailing geopolitics in its neighborhood and religious tensions, India for several years now has been a victim of international terrorism. Several parts of India has been affected by the menace of terrorism. 

Therefore, given India’s vulnerability to international terrorism, it would be pertinent to study, assess and equip with strategies to combat the emerging trends in terrorism. Given Mumbai’s importance as an important financial centre of India and presence of a large and diverse population, it wouldn’t be entirely wrong to say that Mumbai is equally vulnerable to such lone wolf attacks. 

Considering mainly Mumbai’s geographical position, religious diversities and past attacks in the city, Mumbai needs to adapt more vigilant and technologically advanced measures in order to thwart any upcoming threat of terrorist attack. Mumbai has a vast coastline of nearly 150 km which provides an access to vulnerable geographical threats. In the aftermath of 26/11 attacks, Maharashtra Government formed a newly trained unit named [Force One](https://www.theweek.in/theweek/cover/2018/11/10/on-their-toes.html) to combat terrorism. Force One is a specialized, well trained and equipped counter-terrorism unit of the Mumbai Police. Along with it, according to reports, the police forces in Mumbai are well equipped with [Quick Responses Teams (QRTs)](https://www.theweek.in/theweek/cover/2018/11/10/on-their-toes.html) which assist for immediate actions against illegal activities. Given the emergence of lone wolf terrorism in India, it would be pertinent for the state and central governments to ask whether the current counter-terrorism ecosystem is adequate to deal with such threats. Combating lone wolf terrorism requires specialized strategies combining the deployment of technology for mapping potential threats, surveillance and pro-active human intervention. 

Additionally, a major step that could be taken to cease an attack is increasing surveillance in the entire city. According to the [‘Mumbai city surveillance project’ (MCSP)](https://www.thequint.com/tech-and-auto/tech-news/eyes-mumbai-4700-cctv-cameras-traffic-crime-city-surveillance-project-larsen-toubro) of 2016, in a phased manner, Maharashtra Government had planned on setting up approximately 6000 CCTV cameras in the entire city, to keep a watch on illegal and suspicious activities. Another interesting method on this list is the use of [‘drone cameras’](https://localpress.co.in/mumbai/cm-launches-mumbais-cctv-network-4700-cameras-now-watching-city/) for the city. This is seen as a unique step towards surveillance, enabling authorities to keep a check over the city through air. In an interview given to The Indian Express in 2016, the then Maharashtra Police chief Satish Mathur, talked about a suggestion given by the Director General of Civil Aviation (DGCA) to provide Unmanned Aerial Systems (UAS) comprising of a [Unmanned Aircraft (UA), a Remote Pilot Station (RPS), Command and Control (C2) Link, a maintenance system and the operating personnel](https://indianexpress.com/article/india/rules-for-civilian-use-of-drones-soon-uas-dgca-4425884/) for safeguarding the civilians. To ensure security against emerging threats such as lone wolf terrorist attacks, it is important that the process of implementation of the above recommendation be expedited.  

On its part, The Mumbai Police has also made efforts to increase their social media presence in order to reach out to citizens. Their twitter accounts, ‘Mumbai Police’ and ‘CP Mumbai Police’ have been extensively working to enhance citizens’ awareness about terror attacks and other law and order issues. In a city like Mumbai, such social media presence is essential as majority of its residents, especially the youth, are active on social media platforms. If the process of expediting the strategic, tactical and technological measures is implemented, the ability of Mumbai Police to successfully avert possible lone wolf terrorist attacks increases substantially.

Mumbai has witnessed several forms of terrorist attacks since the 1990’s. However, this does not mean that city cannot experience a lone wolf terrorist attack. Given the current diversities, evolving societal behavior and changing socio-political condition of Mumbai, the citizens on their part must undertake individual safety measures. In addition to incorporating usage of advanced technologies to counter lone wolf terrorism, it would be necessary for increasing the interface between public and security forces. In crucial times, a simple step of not sharing fake news and false articles can create a huge impact in helping to curb any illegal activity in the city. 

Responsible behavior and alertness of citizens during crisis situation and active public participation in aiding security forces would help in long way to prevent potential lone wolf attacks.
